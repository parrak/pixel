Why Citron does not replace EHRs

The point is not to replicate scheduling, charting, or claims transmission. The point is to own the operational workflow that those systems do not standardize well.

Why workflow ownership matters

Specialty RCM value depends on who owns work, when they decide, what they escalate, and how fast outcomes improve. That layer is usually fragmented across tools and memory.

Why operational intelligence compounds

Each acquisition creates another operating environment. Citron turns that complexity into reusable process memory, rollup visibility, and standardization leverage.

Why ASC is only the starting point

Workflow definitions, decision memory, and portfolio controls extend to ophthalmology, GI, orthopedics, and future specialty revenue-cycle businesses.
